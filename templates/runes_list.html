About the Elder Futhark

The Elder Futhark is the oldest form of the runic alphabets, used by Germanic tribes between the 2nd and 8th centuries CE. These 24 symbols carry deep spiritual significance and were believed to possess magical properties, serving not only as letters but as keys to divine wisdom.

Each rune represents concepts, forces, and energies that can provide guidance and insight into life's questions. They are associated with Norse deities, natural elements, and archetypal meanings that connect us to ancient Germanic spiritual traditions.

Reading Runes: Some runes can be reversed (merkstave), which often indicates blocked energy, internal challenges, or the shadow aspect of the rune's meaning. Others maintain their meaning regardless of orientation, representing fundamental forces or natural cycles.
